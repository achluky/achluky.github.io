Teaching Philosophy

Active Learning

I believe students learn best when they actively engage with material through discussions, problem-solving, and hands-on activities rather than passive listening.

Collaborative Environment

Creating inclusive learning environments where students feel safe to share ideas, ask questions, and learn from each other's diverse perspectives and experiences.

Growth Mindset

Encouraging students to view challenges as opportunities for growth, emphasizing the importance of effort, persistence, and continuous learning throughout their academic journey.

Technology Integration

Thoughtfully incorporating technology to enhance learning experiences, making education more engaging, accessible, and relevant to today's digital world.

Core Teaching Statement

"Education is not about filling vessels but igniting minds. My role as an educator is to create environments where students discover their potential, develop critical thinking skills, and become lifelong learners who can contribute meaningfully to society."
